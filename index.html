Regulatory Mandates Being Violated

Three Authorities Say PGTs Must Be Paid More. The Federal Govt Ignores All Three.

CPSP — Point No. 6, 09 Jun 2026

College of Physicians & Surgeons Pakistan

"Every trainee must be paid stipend for training at par with that paid by the Federal / Provincial Governments to grade 17 officers. Honorary training is not allowed."

Signed: Prof. Irshad Waheed, Secretary & Acting Registrar, CPSP Karachi. Grade-17 officers receive annual increments. Federal PGTs receive none. Direct violation of mandatory CPSP accreditation requirement.

PM&DC — Public Notice, 29 Aug 2025

Pakistan Medical & Dental Council

"All postgraduate trainings shall be carried out in appropriately remunerated posts and stipendiary positions... Non-compliance shall invite regulatory action, penalties, and potential withdrawal of training recognition."

Under PM&DC Act 2022 & PG Standards & Regulations 2025, Part IV, 11(c). Complaint portal: complaints.pmdc.pk/admin/login

Punjab HF — PHF/ADMIN/3758, 16 Jan 2025

Punjab's Binding Formula (Model for Federal)

"Governor of the Punjab is further pleased to accord sanction that take home stipend for PGs for 1st year PG Trainees shall be equivalent to 90 percent of take home salary of newly inducted regular Medical Officer..."

Punjab links PGT pay to Medical Officer salaries transparently. Federal govt has no such formula — leaving trainees with no legal basis to demand revision within institutions.

Six-Year Record of Neglect

The Documented Timeline

2020

27 January 2020 — Last Revision (PGR & HO)

PM approves 43% increase in PGR stipend: Rs.73,000 → Rs.104,390. HO stipend increased 74%: Rs.40,000 → Rs.69,600. Financial impact to be included in Budget 2020–21 under Head 07-Health.

Ref: F.No.9-24/2016-E-I, signed by Behzad Amir Memon, Section Officer (Budget), Ph:051-9245736. Distributed to: Executive Director PIMS & ED Federal Govt Poly Clinic.

Sep 2020

30 September 2020 — BPS Doctors Get Allowances. PGTs & HOs: Nothing.

President sanctions Non-Practicing Allowance (75% of initial basic pay of 2018) and Special Healthcare Allowance for BPS-17 & 18 doctors at PIMS, FGPC, NIRM, FGH. PGTs and HOs — who work in the same hospitals — receive zero.

Ref: F.No.10-368/2019-H-I, signed by Khalil Ahmed, Deputy Secretary (Budget). Finance Division F.No.2(13)R-2-2018/464, Section Officer Saadia Kanwal.

2021–23

Three Budget Cycles — BPS Revised. PGTs & HOs Excluded Every Year.

Annual federal budgets 2021-22, 2022-23, 2023-24 all include BPS increments and ad-hoc relief. Zero revisions issued for PGTs or HOs. Pakistan's 2023 inflation exceeds 38% — the worst year since independence.

Jul 2024

Punjab Issues KEMU No.9426/KEMU — Revises PGT to Rs.116,708–132,035

KEMU Vice Chancellor issues notification granting 20% Adhoc Relief Allowance on running basic pay w.e.f. 01.07.2024. Punjab PGR Year 1: Rs.111,523 existing → Rs.116,708 revised. Year 5: Rs.125,159 → Rs.132,035. Income tax structure also revised.

Ref: KEMU No.9426/KEMU, dated 27-7-2024. Finance Dept letter No.FD/SR.V 4-1/2024 dated 12.07.2024.

Jan 2025

PM Citizen Portal Complaint Triggers Punjab Health Foundation Response

PGR doctors file complaint No.PU 100126-92338843 dated 14.01.2026 on PM Citizen Portal. Punjab Health Foundation issues Advisory PHF/ADMIN/3758 to Additional Secretary (Finance), Specialised Healthcare & Medical Education Dept Punjab, seeking advice on whether annual increment is admissible to PGR Doctors.

Ref: PHF/ADMIN/3758, Assistant Director General, Punjab Health Foundation, Poonch House 38-Multan Road, Lahore. 16 Jan 2025.

Aug 2025

PM&DC Issues Mandatory Stipend Regulation (29 Aug 2025)

PM&DC Registrar issues Public Notice under PM&DC PG Medical/Dental Education Standards & Regulations 2025 Part IV, 11(c). All PGT trainings must be in appropriately remunerated positions. Private institutions must pay at par with government. Complaint portal: complaints.pmdc.pk/admin/login

Oct 2025

Punjab Again Revises to Rs.123,741–140,901 (KEMU No.11428/KEMU)

10% Adhoc Relief granted w.e.f. 01.07.2025. Punjab PGR Year 1: Rs.116,708 → Rs.123,741. Year 5: Rs.132,035 → Rs.140,901. Federal gap now Rs.36,511/month (Year 5). Federal budget 2025-26 already passed with zero PGT allocation.

Ref: KEMU No.11428/KEMU, 23-10-2025. Finance Dept letter No.FD/SR.V 4-1/2025 dated 10.07.2025.

Jun 2026

CPSP Reiterates Grade-17 Parity Mandate — Point No. 6

CPSP notification lists accreditation requirements for training institutions. Point 6 explicitly states Grade-17 parity is mandatory. Honorary training is not allowed. Federal institutions operating on a 2020 stipend structure are in direct violation of this accreditation requirement.

Ref: Signed by Prof. Irshad Waheed, Secretary & Acting Registrar, CPSP. Forwarded to Prof. Khalid Masood Gondal (Senior VP & Regional Director, CPSP Lahore) and Prof. Mahmood Ayyaz (Director, National Residency Programme, CPSP).

Seven Formal Demands

What We Are Asking the Federal Government to Do

Submitted formally to the Wafaqi Mohtasib Secretariat in June 2026 on behalf of PGTs and House Officers serving in federal healthcare institutions.

01

Investigate 6-Year Non-Revision

Federal Ombudsman to investigate the prolonged failure of M/o NHS,R&C to revise PGT and HO stipends since 2020, despite annual BPS increases and regulatory mandates.

02

Explain Budget Exclusion

Ministry to provide written explanation for excluding PGTs and HOs from every annual budget revision since 2020 while all other federal employee cadres received enhancements.

03

Verify CPSP & PM&DC Compliance

Formally examine whether Rs.104,390 (set in 2020) complies with CPSP Point 6 (Grade-17 parity mandate) and PM&DC PG Standards & Regulations 2025.

04

Establish Revision Formula

Create a transparent, enforceable formula linking PGT and HO stipends to annual BPS pay revisions — equivalent to Punjab's 90%-of-MO-salary structure.

05

Immediate Retrospective Revision

Revise PGT and HO stipends immediately aligned with Grade-17 increments and all adhoc relief granted since July 2020, with retrospective arrears calculated and paid.

06

Close Federal-Punjab Gap

Address the Rs.36,511/month gap between federal and Punjab PGR remuneration (Year 5, 2025) as a matter of administrative equity under the same CPSP regulatory framework.

07

Permanent Annual Review

Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions

Will my hospital know I signed the petition?
No. Signatures are submitted collectively. Your institution name is only used for aggregate statistical representation. Individual trainee names are not disclosed to institutions.
Can I upload my payslip anonymously?
Yes. Tick the "Submit anonymously" box. Your name and identifiers are stripped before the data is used. Only institution type, city, and pay amounts are used in aggregate evidence.
Can House Officers join this campaign?
Absolutely — and this campaign explicitly includes HOs. The January 2020 notification covers both PGRs (Rs.104,390) and House Officers (Rs.69,600). Both have been frozen. Both deserve revision.
Can Medical Officers and private-sector trainees join?
Yes. While the primary focus is on federal institution PGTs and HOs, all trainees and doctors are welcome to sign in solidarity. The more signatures, the stronger the collective representation.
What legal authority does the Ombudsman have?
The Wafaqi Mohtasib can investigate, recommend remedies, and if recommendations are not complied with, can refer the matter to the President of Pakistan. Non-revision of stipends is maladministration under Section 2(3)(i) of the Wafaqi Mohtasib Order 1983.
What happens after we collect 1,000+ signatures?
The complete petition with all signatures, the open letter to the Health Minister, the stipend database evidence, and all formal complaints will be simultaneously submitted to: Federal Ombudsman, Health Minister, NA Standing Committee on Health, PMA, CPSP, PM&DC, and major media outlets.

The Core Issue: The stipend of Postgraduate Residents (PGRs) was fixed at Rs.104,390/month and that of House Officers at Rs.69,600/month by M/o NHS,R&C vide Notification F.No.9-24/2016-E-I dated 27 January 2020. Since that date — across six annual budgets, including Federal Budget 2026-27 — no revision has been made. The Regulatory Breach: CPSP, through its Notification Point No.6 dated 09 June 2026, explicitly mandates: "Every trainee must be paid stipend for training at par with that paid by the Federal/Provincial Governments to grade 17 officers." Grade-17 officers have received multiple annual increments since 2020. Federal PGTs have received none. The same institutions that are accredited by CPSP are in direct violation of CPSP's mandatory accreditation requirement — with the knowledge and implicit approval of your Ministry. PM&DC, in its Public Notice dated 29 August 2025, further mandates that all postgraduate trainings be carried out in appropriately remunerated positions under PG Standards & Regulations 2025, with non-compliance potentially resulting in withdrawal of training recognition. The Evidence of Disparity: Punjab Postgraduate Trainees now receive Rs.123,741–140,901/month (KEMU Notification No.11428/KEMU, October 2025).
